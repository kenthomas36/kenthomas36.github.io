Biography

I've been working on games my whole life. I started working at Ravensburger after I got my Master's Degree in Sociology. The people who tell you that sociology has nothing to do with game design don't have a clue what they're talking about. I started at Ravensburger in the early 60's, helping design the many puzzles they were manufacturing. Though this made the company a lot of money, it was my belief that we needed to challenge players more. Instead of letting them fill out a picture, we should give them playful experiences that would activate their imagination. Anything else would be a waste of time. In 1977, when the company finally split itself into a Books and Games division, it was under my supervision that classics like "Rabbit and Hedgehog", "Enchanted Forest" and "Master Labyrinth" came to be. Although I see all of my games as my children, it was my work at the long-forgotten Luck & Sense Games that I am most proud of.
